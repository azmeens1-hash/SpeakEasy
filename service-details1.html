Course Overview

Why Learn Arabic with SpeakEasy?

Arabic is spoken by over 400 million native speakers across 22 countries and is an official language of the United Nations. It is the language of Islam, ancient scholarship, contemporary diplomacy, and one of the world's fastest-growing economies — with the Gulf region projected to be a $6 trillion economy by 2030.

Our Arabic programme is structured around Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) as your foundation, with dedicated dialect tracks for Egyptian, Gulf, and Levantine Arabic. Every lesson is led by a certified native tutor in small groups of 6 students.

"من تعلّم لغةً أتقن ثقافةً" — "Whoever learns a language masters a culture." Our graduates now work in Dubai, Riyadh, Cairo, and Amman — and they started exactly where you are today.

We've designed the curriculum to tackle what truly makes Arabic challenging: the script, the root system, and the gap between written and spoken Arabic. By week 4 you'll be reading Arabic script. By week 8, you'll be holding basic conversations. By week 16, you'll understand native speakers across multiple dialects.

Learning Outcomes

What You'll Master in Arabic

Eight core Arabic skills developed across 16 weeks of live, native-led practice — covering script, grammar, speech, and culture.

Arabic Script Mastery (الكتابة)
Read and write all 28 Arabic letters in their four positional forms. Master short vowels (harakat), sun and moon letters, and connected script — within just 4 weeks.
The Arabic Root System (جذر)
Unlock the genius of Arabic morphology — learn how 3-letter roots expand into hundreds of words. Once you crack the root system, your vocabulary grows exponentially.
Spoken Arabic — Your Dialect Track
Choose Egyptian, Gulf, or Levantine Arabic as your conversational dialect. You'll speak it in every session — roleplay scenarios in souks, offices, and homes.
MSA Listening & Al Jazeera Comprehension
Understand Arabic news broadcasts, formal speeches, and documentaries. We use Al Jazeera, BBC Arabic, and MBC as real listening materials from Week 6 onwards.
Islamic & Cultural Arabic Intelligence
Understand Quranic phrases, Islamic social expressions, Arabic proverbs, and the unwritten codes of Arab hospitality and culture.
1,000+ Arabic Words in Context (مفردات)
Vocabulary taught through root families, not word lists. By Week 16 you'll deploy 1,000+ Arabic words instinctively in both formal and conversational contexts.
Business & Diplomatic Arabic
Negotiate, present, and correspond professionally in MSA. Essential for professionals working in the Gulf, international development, diplomacy, or Arabic-speaking markets.
Arabic Pronunciation & Makhaarij (مخارج)
Master the unique Arabic sounds: the 'ayn (ع), ghain (غ), ha (ح), and emphatic consonants. Our AI phonetics coach gives real-time feedback on every Arabic sound you produce.
Course Structure

Arabic Week-by-Week Curriculum

16 weeks structured into 4 phases — from your first Arabic letter to fluent conversation in MSA and your chosen dialect.

01
Week 1–2: الحروف — The Arabic Alphabet & Script System
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Foundation
▾
Master all 28 Arabic letters, their four positional forms, and connect them into real Arabic words from day one.
  • The 28 Arabic letters — names, sounds, and shapes
  • Positional forms: كـ / ـكـ / ـك — same letter, four positions
  • Sun letters vs moon letters and the definite article ال
  • Short vowels (harakat): فَتحة، كَسرة، ضَمة
  • Reading practice: your first full Arabic sentences
02
Week 3–4: أول كلمات — First Vocabulary & Greetings
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Foundation
▾
  • Essential greetings: السلام عليكم، مرحباً، كيف حالك؟
  • Numbers 1–100 in Arabic (cardinal and ordinal)
  • Days, months, and telling time in MSA
  • Introducing yourself: اسمي، أنا من، أنا أعمل في
  • Roleplay: first conversation at an Arab market (السوق)
03
Week 5–6: الجذر — Cracking the Arabic Root System
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Grammar
▾
The Arabic root system (الجذر الثلاثي) is the key to exponential vocabulary growth.
  • Trilateral root families: ك-ت-ب (writing) → كَتَبَ، كِتاب، كاتِب، مَكتوب، مَكتَب
  • Pattern recognition: mastering the 10 Arabic verb forms (أوزان الأفعال)
  • Nominal patterns: مَفعَل، فَعّال، فَعيل — understanding word structure
  • Practice: deriving 50 new words from 10 roots
04
Week 7: الفعل — Arabic Verbs & Sentence Structure
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Grammar
▾
  • Past tense (الماضي) and present tense (المضارع) conjugation for all persons
  • VSO vs SVO word order — Arabic sentence flexibility
  • Dual (المثنى) and broken plural (الجمع التكسيري)
  • Negation: لا، لم، لن، ما — four ways to say "no"
  • Roleplay: describing your work and daily routine in MSA
05
Week 9–11: اللهجة — Your Dialect Track (Egyptian / Gulf / Levantine)
9 sessions · 4.5 hrs total
Dialect Immersion
▾
Immerse in your chosen dialect — all three tracks run in parallel.
  • Egyptian: قاهرة street Arabic — café conversations, negotiating, humour.
  • Gulf: خليجي formal and social Arabic. Business greetings, hospitality expressions.
  • Levantine: شامي daily life Arabic — Amman, Beirut, Damascus register.
  • Dialect vs MSA: when to switch registers in real life
  • Arabic proverbs, slang, and cultural expression by region
06
Week 12: الثقافة — Arab Culture, Etiquette & Islamic Expressions
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Culture
▾
  • Islamic expressions in daily Arabic: إن شاء الله، الحمد لله، بسم الله
  • Arab hospitality culture: guest etiquette, declining food, accepting tea
  • Gender and formality in Arabic address — regional differences
  • Arabic media: Al Jazeera news listening with comprehension tasks
07
Week 13–15: الطلاقة — Fluency Sprints & Advanced Arabic
9 sessions · 4.5 hrs total
Advanced
▾
  • Advanced verb forms: Form V–X derivations and their meanings
  • Arabic conditional: لو كان، إذا كان — hypothetical and real conditions
  • Debate and argumentation in MSA — formal speech patterns
  • Arabic writing tasks: emails, reports, and formal correspondence
  • 30-second improvised Arabic speech drills with AI feedback
08
Week 16: التقييم — Final Evaluation & CEFR Certificate
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Certification
▾
Your programme culminates in a live 20-minute Arabic conversation with a native-speaking evaluator.
  • Mock oral evaluation in Week 15 with full written feedback
  • Arabic fluency report: strengths, dialect progress, and next-step recommendations
  • CEFR-aligned digital certificate (PDF + LinkedIn sharable)
  • Pathway guidance: advanced courses, Arabic study-abroad programmes, OPIc Arabic test
