Course Overview

Why Learn Italian with SpeakEasy?

Italian is spoken by over 65 million people across Italy, Switzerland, San Marino, and diaspora communities worldwide. It is the language of Leonardo, Dante, Verdi, and Ferrari — a language so musicale that it gave the world the vocabulary of classical music itself: piano, forte, allegro, soprano.

Our Italian programme puts real conversation at the heart of every session. Each class is led live by a certified native tutor — from Rome, Milan, Naples, or Florence — in groups of just 6 students. You'll roleplay authentic Italian scenarios: ordering at a trattoria, navigating Florence, negotiating at a Milanese business meeting.

"Benvenuto! In 12 settimane passerai dal tuo primo 'ciao' a conversazioni complete con madrelingua. Questa è la nostra promessa."
— Giulia Conti, Lead Italian Tutor

We cover all 12 Italian levels from A1 (complete beginner) to C2 (native mastery), with dedicated tracks for CILS/CELI exam preparation, Business Italian for fashion and luxury industries, and a special Opera & Culture track for enthusiasts.

Course Structure

Italian Week-by-Week Curriculum

12 weeks structured into 4 phases — each building directly on the last, from "Ciao!" to full Italian fluency.

01
Week 1–2: Primi Passi — First Steps in Italian
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Foundation
▾
Italian phonetics, the alphabet, greetings, and essential phrases.
  • Italian sounds: double consonants, rolled R, vowel clarity
  • Saluti: ciao, buongiorno, buonasera, come stai?
  • Numbers, time, days of the week
  • Essere and avere
  • Roleplay: ordering espresso at a Roma bar
02
Week 3: Conversazione Quotidiana — Daily Italian Life
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Foundation
▾
Introduce yourself and talk about your interests.
  • Present tense: verbs in -are, -ere, -ire
  • Gender in Italian — masculine and feminine nouns
  • Italian question words: chi, cosa, quando, dove
  • Mi piace / non mi piace
03
Week 4–5: Passato & Futuro — Past & Future Tenses
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Grammar
▾
Master the key past tenses and the future.
  • Passato prossimo: avere vs essere
  • Irregular past participles
  • Imperfetto: childhood memories and scene-setting
  • Futuro semplice
04
Week 6: Congiuntivo & Condizionale
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Grammar
▾
The congiuntivo separates intermediate from advanced.
  • Congiuntivo presente: penso che, spero che
  • Condizionale: vorrei, potrebbe, sarebbe meglio
  • Expressing wishes, doubts, and emotions
05
Week 7: L'Italia nella Vita Reale — Italy in Real Life
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Immersion
▾
Navigate real-world Italian scenarios.
  • Travel: trains, airports, hotels in Italian cities
  • Dining: reading Italian menus, ordering wine
  • Northern vs Southern Italy: vocabulary and cultural differences
06
Week 9: Modi di Dire, Gesti & Cultura
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
Immersion
▾
Sound authentically Italian.
  • 20 essential Italian idioms
  • Italian hand gestures and what each one means
  • La bella figura — Italy's unwritten social code
  • Italian cinema session: Federico Fellini
07
Week 10–11: Sprint di Fluidità — Fluency Sprints
6 sessions · 3 hrs total
Advanced
▾
High-intensity Italian conversation practice.
  • 30-second improvised speech drills
  • Debate topics: politica, gastronomia, arte, moda
  • Full vocabulary review: 1,100+ Italian words
  • CILS oral exam simulation
08
Week 12: Valutazione Finale & Certificato CILS
3 sessions · 1.5 hrs total
CILS Certification
▾
A 20-minute live Italian conversation with a CILS-certified examiner.
  • Mock CILS oral exam in Week 11
  • Personalised Italian fluency report
  • CEFR-aligned digital certificate (PDF + LinkedIn)
  • CILS/CELI exam registration guidance
